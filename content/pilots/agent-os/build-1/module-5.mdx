# Module 5: Wishes, Edge Cases, and Surprises

This is the last module in Build 1. It covers forward-looking feedback -- not what went wrong, but what you'd want next, what the tool couldn't handle, and what worked better than you expected.

These three categories produce different kinds of signal. All three matter for Build 2 planning.

## Wishes

A wish is something you wanted vault-intake to do that it doesn't do, or something you'd change about how it currently works.

Wishes are less reliable than friction and surprises as product signals -- everyone wants more features. But a wish that comes from actual use ("I kept wanting to do X and had to do it manually") is different from a theoretical wish ("it would be cool if..."). Report the former.

Examples of high-signal wishes:
- "I wanted to capture a PDF directly, not via a URL, but that wasn't possible."
- "I wanted to add my own annotations during capture, not just in the empty My Notes section after."
- "I wanted the summary length to be adjustable per capture, not a global setting."

## Edge cases

An edge case is a situation where vault-intake didn't handle something well, produced wrong output, or where you weren't sure what to do.

Edge cases are higher-signal than wishes. They reveal where the tool's assumptions don't match real usage.

Examples:
- A source type the tool doesn't support (yet)
- A content format that produced a low-quality capture
- A step in the workflow that was ambiguous or had unexpected behavior
- A vault configuration combination that didn't work as documented

Report edge cases with as much specificity as you have. "I tried to capture an X, here's what happened" is useful. "I think it might have trouble with X" is less so.

## Surprises

A surprise is anything that worked differently than you expected, in either direction.

**Good surprises:** Things that worked better than anticipated. Features you didn't know were there. Behavior that solved a problem you hadn't mentioned.

**Bad surprises:** Things that worked, technically, but produced output that didn't match your mental model of what "working" should look like.

<Insight>
Surprises are the most valuable signal in this module. They reveal gaps between documentation (or lack of it) and actual expectations. A bad surprise that multiple pilots report independently is almost certainly something that needs either a fix or clearer documentation. A good surprise that pilots didn't expect might be a feature that needs more visibility.
</Insight>

## What happens with this feedback

Every response to this module is read directly. Items that appear across multiple pilots become Build 2 candidates. Items that are specific to one configuration are logged for documentation improvements.

If something you report changes a design decision, you'll hear about it. That's not a promise for every item -- some wishes will be out of scope, some edge cases will be low priority. But the patterns matter and they drive real decisions.
