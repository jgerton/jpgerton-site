# Build 2: Scaling and Sustaining

Build 1 got you from theory to your first 100 members. Build 2 covers what happens after people join: how to keep them, how to make the paid tier worth it, how to prevent churn, how to stack revenue beyond subscriptions, and how to communicate with proof instead of promises.

## What's In Build 2

- **Module 5: Onboarding That Sticks.** A 7-day welcome sequence, Day 1 activation design, 90-day integration path, and early warning system for churn. Built from a 57,000-person study showing automated welcomes alone do nothing.

- **Module 6: Live Sessions That Justify the Price.** Format selection (Q&A, workshop, office hours, hot seat, co-working), async access for members who can't attend, and the challenge framework that converts 25-45% of completers to paid members.

- **Module 7: Keeping Them (Churn Prevention).** Your churn dashboard (4 metrics), re-engagement system, monthly value reminders, and why adding more content can actually increase churn.

- **Module 8: The Revenue Ladder.** Layered monetization from subscriptions to challenges to digital products to premium tiers to high-ticket coaching. Real revenue scenarios for $5K-10K/mo.

- **Module 9: Proof-Based Communication.** The proof ledger, the Proof > Context > Small Ask pattern, the conversion flywheel, and the second email framework. Anchored to the real story of Allie Janoch's cold email that led to a $2.5M investment.

## How Build 2 Connects to Build 1

Build 1 exercises created your community structure: tiers, Start Here module, gamification, seed content, growth channels. Build 2 exercises build on those foundations. You'll reference your Day 1 activation metric from Module 3, your pricing from Module 2, and your growth channels from Module 4.

If you haven't done Build 1's exercises, start there. Build 2 assumes you have a configured community with at least a few members.

## Evidence Base

Every claim in Build 2 went through the same three-step verification process as Build 1: research, verify, try to disprove. When we couldn't verify a commonly cited stat (like "52% cite lack of engagement" or "20% to 2.4% churn with annual billing"), we flagged it honestly rather than repeating it as fact.

New research sources in Build 2 include a peer-reviewed Wikipedia onboarding study (n=57,084), Churnkey State of Retention 2025, Amplitude activation research, and updated CommuniPass challenge data showing 70-80% completion rates (higher than the 40-60% we cited in Build 1).
