# The Freemium Community Playbook

## Build Status

This playbook is being built in public. You're reading Build 1, which covers the full launch arc: why freemium works (and when it doesn't), configuring your Skool, preparing to launch, and getting your first 100 members. Future builds will add modules as they're researched, verified, and ready.

I'm building this as I go, implementing each module on my own Skool community (You Craft and AI Helps) alongside you. I don't have a success story to point to yet. I have the same questions you do, backed by the research I did to answer them. What you're reading is the result of that research, and what we'll build together is the proof.

Each build ships when it's ready, not all at once. Co-pilots follow along, run the exercises on their own communities, and report back what works and what doesn't. Your results become part of the playbook. You're not consuming a course. You're participating in an experiment.

Current status:
- Build 1: Modules 1-4 (from theory through first 100 members) -- ready
- Modules 5-8: Outlined, shipping in Build 2 (onboarding, live sessions, churn, revenue ladder)
- Evidence base: verified claims with sources, antagonist-tested (we actively tried to disprove our own claims and corrected where the evidence disagreed)
- 12 implementation exercises across 4 modules, from thinking exercises to things you build in your actual Skool

## For Co-Pilots

If you're here because you want to follow along and test this playbook on your own community, here's everything you need to know.

**What you're signing up for:**
- Read each build as it ships
- Run the exercises on your community (or your planned community)
- Share your results, numbers, and feedback in the discussion thread for each build
- Tell us what's missing, what's confusing, and what doesn't match your experience

**What you're NOT signing up for:**
- No weekly meetings, no mandatory check-ins, no deadlines
- No obligation to share anything you're not comfortable sharing
- No pressure to have a community already (starting from zero is a valid data point)

**How to participate:**
1. Read the build
2. Do the exercises (each one has a clear deliverable)
3. Post your results in the Build discussion thread on Skool
4. Fill out the short feedback form linked at the end of each build (2 minutes)
5. React to other co-pilots' posts. Your experience might confirm or challenge theirs.

**What you get:**
- Early access to every build before it's finalized
- Your feedback directly shapes the playbook (you'll see your input reflected in future builds)
- A cohort of other community builders working through the same challenges
- Credit in the final playbook if your data or insights make it in

**How feedback works:**
- Discussion thread (Skool post): open conversation, reactions, questions, "this worked / this didn't"
- Feedback form (linked at end of each build): open text boxes so you tell us what matters, not what we think to ask
- DMs welcome for anything you don't want to share publicly

## How We Built This

Every claim in this playbook went through a three-step process. First, we researched it using academic studies, industry benchmark reports, and practitioner data from real Skool communities. Second, we verified it by tracing numbers back to their original sources and dropping anything we couldn't confirm. Third, we tried to disprove it. We actively searched for counter-evidence, failure cases, and situations where the advice breaks down. When the evidence disagreed with popular advice (like the "100 members in 30 days" timeline or the "$29-49/mo sweet spot"), we went with the evidence.

What survived that process is in the playbook. What didn't is either gone or flagged honestly in the "What we don't know yet" sections at the end of each module. Those sections aren't filler. They're the specific questions we're asking co-pilots to help answer with real data.

Every module ends with a sources section listing where each claim came from. If you want to check our work, it's all there.

## Outcome Promise

Turn your expertise into a $2K-5K/mo community business with a two-tier Skool setup.

## Who This Is For

Creators, solopreneurs, and practitioners who have expertise people ask them about but no scalable way to monetize it. You might be:

- Teaching through free content (YouTube, blog, social) and ready to capture recurring revenue
- Running a paid community that's stalling or churning
- Considering Skool but unsure how to structure free vs. paid
- Already on Skool with a free community and no clear path to monetization

## What "Done" Looks Like

By the end of this playbook, you have:

- A single Skool community running two tiers (free and paid)
- Free tier converting 3-10% of active members to paid (industry benchmark), higher with challenge funnels (8-15%)
- Paid tier at a price point matched to your niche (real communities range from $1-500/mo; see Module 2 for evidence-based pricing)
- A content rhythm you can sustain (3-5 posts/week, not daily burnout)
- An onboarding flow that activates 30%+ of new members on Day 1
- Churn under 8% monthly (community-driven retention, not content treadmill)
- A weekly live session format that justifies the paid tier
- Clear metrics you track to know what's working

## Prerequisites

- An area of expertise people already ask you about
- Willingness to show up consistently for 90 days
- A Skool account (Hobby plan is fine to start, $9/mo)
- No audience required (the playbook covers growth from zero)
